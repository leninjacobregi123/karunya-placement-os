# Percentages — Going Deeper

## Successive Percentage Changes

When a value changes by multiple percentages in sequence, you CANNOT simply add them.

**Formula for successive changes:**
If value changes by A% then B%:
New value = Original × (1 + A/100) × (1 + B/100)

**Equivalent single change:**
Equivalent % = (A + B + A×B/100)%

**Examples:**
1. Price increases by 20%, then by 10%:
   - Single equivalent: 20 + 10 + (20×10)/100 = 32% increase
   - Or: × 1.20 × 1.10 = × 1.32

2. Population decreases by 10%, then by 20%:
   - Equivalent: -10 + (-20) + (-10)(-20)/100 = -10 - 20 + 2 = -28%
   - Or: × 0.90 × 0.80 = × 0.72

3. Price increased 25%, decreased 20%:
   - Equivalent: 25 + (-20) + 25(-20)/100 = 5 - 5 = 0%
   - So the price returns to original! (But not always!)

4. Price increased 20%, decreased 10%, increased 10%:
   - Equivalent: 20 + (-10) + 10 + 20×(-10)/100 + 20×10/100 + (-10)×10/100 + 20×(-10)×10/10000
   - = 20% (let's verify: × 1.20 × 0.90 × 1.10 = × 1.188 = +18.8%)

## Profit, Loss & Percentage

**Profit = Selling Price - Cost Price**
**Profit % = (Profit / Cost Price) × 100**

**Loss = Cost Price - Selling Price**
**Loss % = (Loss / Cost Price) × 100**

**Key formula:** If a shopkeeper gains p% and sells for ₹Y:
- Cost Price = Y / (1 + p/100) = Y × 100 / (100 + p)

Example: A book is sold at ₹180 with 20% profit.
CP = 180 / 1.20 = ₹150

**If sold at a loss of l%:**
- Cost Price = Y × 100 / (100 - l)

Example: An item sold at ₹90 with 10% loss.
CP = 90 / 0.90 = ₹100

## Population Growth/Decay

Population after n years at r% annual growth:
P = P₀ × (1 + r/100)ⁿ

Population after n years at r% annual decay:
P = P₀ × (1 - r/100)ⁿ

Example: City population was 50,000, grows 4% per year.
After 3 years: 50,000 × (1.04)³ = 50,000 × 1.1249 ≈ 56,245

## Common Percentage Scenarios

### Scenario 1: Vote/Percentage Problems
"In an election, candidate A gets 55% of votes and wins by 20,000 votes. Find total voters."

Winner: 55% of total
Loser: 45% of total
Difference: 10% of total = 20,000
Total = 200,000

### Scenario 2: Exam Pass Marks
"In an exam, 40% is pass marks. Student gets 120 marks and fails by 40 marks."

Pass marks = 120 + 40 = 160
160 = 40% of total
Total = 160/0.40 = 400

### Scenario 3: Two Successive Price Changes
A product costs ₹1000. Price increases 10%, then decreases 10%. Net effect?

1000 × 1.10 × 0.90 = 1000 × 0.99 = ₹990
Net: 1% decrease!

**Key insight:** Successive equal percentage increase and decrease always gives a net decrease of:
Decrease% = (x/100)² (for equal x%)

### Scenario 4: A is what % more/less than B?

A > B: (A-B)/B × 100 = ___% more
B > A: (B-A)/A × 100 = ____% more (B)

Example: A=150, B=100
A is 50% more than B
B is 33.3% less than A

**NOTE:** The denominators differ! This is the most common trap.

## Multi-Step Problems

### Example: Salary Adjustment

A salary of ₹50,000 is:
1. Increased by 20% → ₹50,000 × 1.20 = ₹60,000
2. Then decreased by 15% → ₹60,000 × 0.85 = ₹51,000

Net change: +2% (from ₹50,000 to ₹51,000)

### Example: Tax Calculation

A product's price including 10% tax is ₹1,100. What is the price without tax?

Price + 10% tax = 110% of price = ₹1,100
Price = ₹1,100 / 1.10 = ₹1,000

### Example: Composition Problem

A mixture is 40% alcohol. If 10 liters of water is added, the new mixture is 30% alcohol. Find initial volume.

Let initial volume = V liters
Alcohol = 0.40V (stays constant, only water is added)
New volume = V + 10
New mixture: 0.40V / (V + 10) = 0.30
0.40V = 0.30V + 3
0.10V = 3
V = 30 liters

## Complex Problem

A person's salary increased by 20% for one year. The next year, he was given a 10% cut. In the third year, he was given another 15% cut. His salary is now ₹6,46,000. What was his original salary?

Let original = X
After +20%: X × 1.20
After -10%: X × 1.20 × 0.90 = X × 1.08
After -15%: X × 1.08 × 0.85 = X × 0.918

So X × 0.918 = ₹6,46,000
X = ₹6,46,000 / 0.918 ≈ ₹7,03,703.70

Check: 7,03,704 → +20% = 8,44,445 → -10% = 7,60,000 → -15% = ₹6,46,000 ✓

## Interview Tips

1. **Convert to fractions first** when fractions are clean (25%, 33%, 50%, 75%, etc.). Much faster than decimals.
2. **Use multipliers:** +20% → × 1.2, -25% → × 0.75. Chain them.
3. **Watch for trap:** "A is 20% more than B. How much % is B less than A?" B is not 20% less! Use B/A = 1/1.2 ≈ 83.3%, so B is 16.7% less.
4. **If the question has "x% more" in a chain,** set up the final equation with multipliers.
5. **Successive equal change +x% then -x% always gives net decrease of (x/100)²%.**
6. **In exams, eliminate wrong answers first** — many wrong options result from using wrong base or not chaining multipliers.
