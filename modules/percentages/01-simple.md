# Percentages — 01. The Core Idea

## The Core Formula

Percentage = (Part / Total) × 100

This is the ONLY formula you need to memorize. Every percentage problem reduces to this.

## Example 1: Finding the Percentage

**Question:** In a class of 40 students, 25 are women. What percentage are women?

Part = 25, Total = 40
Percentage = (25/40) × 100 = 62.5%

## Example 2: Finding the Part

**Question:** What is 15% of 200?

15 = (Part/200) × 100
Part = (15 × 200) / 100 = 30

## Example 3: Finding the Total

**Question:** 40 is 20% of what number?

40 = 20% × Total
Total = (40 × 100) / 20 = 200

## Mental Math Tricks

- 10% = divide by 10
- 25% = divide by 4
- 50% = divide by 2
- 75% = 3 × 25%

Example: What is 30% of 160?
10% = 16 (160 ÷ 10)
30% = 16 × 3 = 48

## When to Use Percentages

Look for these clues:
- "___% of ___" → multiply the % (as decimal) by the number
- "___ is what % of ___?" → divide and multiply by 100
- "Increased/decreased by ___%" → use the % value as a multiplier

## Interview Strategy

1. Identify what you know: Part, Total, or Percentage
2. Plug into the formula
3. For quick calculation, convert % to decimal:
   - 25% = 0.25
   - 75% = 0.75
   - 120% = 1.20

## Practice Problem

A shirt costs ₹800. During sale, it's discounted by 25%. What's the final price?

Solution:
Discount = 25% of 800 = 0.25 × 800 = ₹200
Final price = 800 - 200 = ₹600

Or shortcut: Final = 800 × 0.75 = ₹600 (keep 75% of original)
