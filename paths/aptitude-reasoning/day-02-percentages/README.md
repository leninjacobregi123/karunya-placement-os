# Day 02: Percentages

## Learning Objectives

- Understand what percentage means (x per 100)
- Convert percentages to fractions, decimals, and back
- Calculate a percentage of a number quickly
- Solve problems involving percentage increase and decrease
- Handle successive percentage changes (they are NOT additive)
- Memorise and use common percentage–fraction shortcuts

## 1. What Is a Percentage?

A percentage means "out of 100." The symbol % literally means "per centum" (per hundred).

- 50% means 50 out of 100, or 50/100
- 7% means 7 out of 100, or 7/100
- 150% means 150 out of 100 = 1.5 times the original

Real-world example: If a shop says "20% off," it means you pay only 80% of the original price.

## 2. Converting Between %, Fractions, and Decimals

The key idea is the same: %, fraction, and decimal are three ways to express the same part-of-a-whole idea.

% to fraction: Remove the % symbol and divide by 100.
  - 25% → 25/100 → 1/4
  - 60% → 60/100 → 3/5

% to decimal: Divide by 100 (shift the decimal point two places left).
  - 25% → 0.25
  - 60% → 0.60
  - 150% → 1.50

Fraction to %: Multiply by 100.
  - 3/4 × 100 = 75%
  - 2/5 × 100 = 40%

## 3. Calculating % of a Number

Formula: X% of Y = (X/100) × Y

Shortcut: Convert X% to a decimal first, then multiply.

Examples:
- 35% of 200 = 0.35 × 200 = 70
- 8% of 350 = 0.08 × 350 = 28
- 125% of 40 = 1.25 × 40 = 50

## 4. Percentage Increase and Decrease

Percentage increase happens when a value goes up. Percentage decrease happens when a value goes down.

Formula: % change = ((New Value - Old Value) / Old Value) × 100

- Example: Salary increases from Rs 30,000 to Rs 36,000
  % increase = ((36000 - 30000) / 30000) × 100 = 20%

- Example: Price drops from Rs 200 to Rs 150
  % decrease = ((150 - 200) / 200) × 100 = -25%

## 5. The % Change Formula

The most common mistake: using the wrong base (denominator). The OLD value (the starting value) is always the denominator.

Example: A city's population was 50,000 last year. This year it is 55,000.
% change = ((55000 - 50000) / 50000) × 100 = 10% INCREASE

Tricky example: Price goes up 20%, then down 20%. Is it back to original?
Start: Rs 100 → After +20%: 100 × 1.20 = Rs 120 → After -20%: 120 × 0.80 = Rs 96
NOT back to 100! The base changed between the two steps.

## 6. Successive Percentage Changes (NOT Additive!)

Two successive changes of ±a% and ±b% do NOT equal (a ± b)%.

Method 1: Step-by-step multiplication
- Start with 100 (easy mental base)
- Apply first change as a multiplier
- Apply second change as a multiplier

Method 2: Net % change formula
Successive changes of a% and b% give a net change of: a + b + (a × b)/100 %

Examples:
- Increase 10%, then increase another 10%: Net = 10 + 10 + (10 × 10)/100 = 21% increase
- Increase 20%, then decrease 15%: Net = 20 + (-15) + (20 × -15)/100 = 2% increase
