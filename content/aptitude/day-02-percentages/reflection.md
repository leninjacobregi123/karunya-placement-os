# Day 02 Reflection Questions

## Think about your learning

1. When does a percentage decrease from X% followed by an X% increase NOT bring you back to the starting number? Can you explain why with a concrete example?

2. Why are common fraction-to-percentage shortcuts (like 1/4 = 25%, 1/3 = 33.33%) essential for placement exam speed? Pick two you struggled with today and write them down.

3. In the price discount problem, the first discount was 20% and the second was 10%. The total discount was NOT 30%. What is the general rule: can two successive percentage changes ever be added directly?

4. A student scored 70% on a 60-mark test and 68.75% on an 80-mark test. In absolute marks, which test was better? What does this tell you about comparing percentages from different totals?

5. If a population grows by p% one year and falls by p% the next, will it end up higher, lower, or the same? Explain with a small number (try p = 10 or p = 5).
