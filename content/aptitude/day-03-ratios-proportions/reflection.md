# Reflection — Day 03: Ratios and Proportions

1. Which step do you usually skip when reducing a ratio — finding the HCF first, or dividing both terms at once? Does skipping it cause calculation errors, or do you prefer to simplify step by step?

2. When combining two ratios with a common term, why must you use LCM? What happens mathematically if you just add the ratios instead?

3. In the mean proportion formula sqrt(a x c), why can't you just take (a + c) / 2 (the arithmetic mean) instead? What is actually different about the geometric approach?

4. In real-world problems (recipes, mixtures, map scales), what is the most important first step before applying any formula? Have you lost marks in exams by rushing this step?

5. When the answer to a quiz question doesn't match any option, what debugging strategy do you use? Do you recheck your HCF, verify the cross-multiplication, or re-read the question for misread numbers?
