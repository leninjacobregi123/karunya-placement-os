For word problems: (1) underline all numbers and their units. (2) Write the ratio using those numbers. (3) Check if the ratio is part-to-part or part-to-whole. (4) Set up the proportion with cross-multiplication. (5) Always verify your answer by checking: do the ratio terms still hold? Does the sum match the total? If any check fails, revisit step (1) — you likely misread the question.

For the third proportion of a and b: the formula is c = b^2/a. Think of it as: if a:b = b:c, then c = b^2/a. For practice, the third proportion of 6 and 9 is 81/6 = 13.5 because 6:9 = 9:13.5 (both = 2:3).
