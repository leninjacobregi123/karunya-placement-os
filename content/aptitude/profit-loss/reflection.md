# Reflection Questions: Profit and Loss

1. If you buy an item for ₹100 and sell it at 10% profit, then buy the same item again for the selling price and sell it at 10% loss, do you break even? Calculate the final value step by step.

2. A shopkeeper marks an item 20% above CP and then gives a 10% discount. What is the net profit or loss percentage?

3. Why is the common saying "equal profit and loss percentages always mean loss" always true? Try proving it algebraically with profit p% and loss p% on two items at the same SP.

4. A product goes through three price changes: +20%, -15%, +10%. What is the net change? Use the multiplier method.

5. If you know only that a product's price went from ₹X to ₹Y, can you determine if it was a profit or loss? What additional information do you need?

## Key Takeaways

- Profit% and Loss% are ALWAYS on CP
- Successive changes = multiply (1 + p₁/100) × (1 + p₂/100)
- Equal profit% and loss% on same SP always gives loss of p²%
- Discount is on MP, profit is on CP — different bases!
