# Profit and Loss

## Learning Objectives

- Understand Cost Price (CP) and Selling Price (SP)
- Calculate profit and loss percentages
- Find CP or SP when one value and percentage is given
- Handle successive profit/loss scenarios
- Understand discount and marked price concepts

## 1. Fundamentals

**Cost Price (CP):** Price at which you buy something
**Selling Price (SP):** Price at which you sell it

- **Profit:** SP > CP → Profit = SP - CP
- **Loss:** CP > SP → Loss = CP - SP

## 2. Key Formulas

```
Profit% = (Profit / CP) × 100
Loss% = (Loss / CP) × 100

SP = CP × (1 + profit%/100) when there's profit
SP = CP × (1 - loss%/100) when there's loss

CP = SP / (1 + profit%/100) when given SP and profit%
CP = SP / (1 - loss%/100) when given SP and loss%
```

**CRITICAL:** Profit% and Loss% are ALWAYS calculated on CP, not SP.

### Example 1: Simple Profit
You buy a pen for ₹20 and sell it for ₹25.
- Profit = 25 - 20 = ₹5
- Profit% = (5/20) × 100 = 25%

### Example 2: Simple Loss
You buy a shirt for ₹500 and sell it for ₹400.
- Loss = 500 - 400 = ₹100
- Loss% = (100/500) × 100 = 20%

### Example 3: Finding CP
You sell a book for ₹270 with 10% profit. What was the CP?
- SP = CP × 1.10
- 270 = CP × 1.10
- CP = 270/1.10 = ₹245.45

### Example 4: Finding SP
You buy an item for ₹800 and want 15% profit.
- SP = 800 × 1.15 = ₹920

## 3. Successive Profit/Loss

When a product goes through multiple transactions, multiply the factors:

Example: A buys for ₹100, sells to B at 20% profit. B sells to C at 10% loss.
- A to B: SP = 100 × 1.20 = ₹120
- B to C: SP = 120 × 0.90 = ₹108

Overall change: 100 → 108 → **₹8 profit (8%)**

Net formula: (1 + p₁/100) × (1 + p₂/100) × ... = final factor

## 4. Discount and Marked Price

**Marked Price (MP):** Price before any discount
**Discount:** Reduction on MP
**SP = MP - Discount**

Discount% = (Discount / MP) × 100

Example: A shirt marked ₹800 with 25% discount.
- Discount = 25% of 800 = ₹200
- SP = 800 - 200 = ₹600

### Successive Discounts

Two successive discounts of x% and y% ≠ (x + y)%.
Use: MP × (1 - x/100) × (1 - y/100) = SP

Example: 20% discount followed by 10% discount on ₹1000:
SP = 1000 × 0.80 × 0.90 = ₹720
Total discount = ₹280 = 28% (not 30%!)

## 5. Dishonest Shopkeeper Problems

Sometimes a shopkeeper uses false weights:
- Sells at CP but uses 900g instead of 1kg
- Effectively gains: (1000 - 900)/900 × 100 = 11.11%

2. Solve examples (5 min) — work through Examples 1-4 yourself
3. Practice problems (12 min) — try all 5 problems
4. Check answers (3 min) — verify your work

## Common Mistakes

1. **Wrong base:** Always use CP for % calculation, not SP
2. **Adding successive %:** Two 20% discounts ≠ 40% off
3. **Equal profit/loss:** If you sell two items at same SP with equal % profit and loss, you ALWAYS lose overall. The loss% = (x/10)^2%
