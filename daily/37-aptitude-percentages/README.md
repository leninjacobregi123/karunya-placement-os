# Percentages

**Duration:** 30 minutes  **Difficulty:** Beginner  **Track:** Aptitude + Reasoning

**Learning Goal:** Master percentage calculations, conversions, and applications in placement tests.

## What You'll Learn

Percentages are a way of expressing a number as a fraction of 100. The word "percent" literally means "per hundred." Understanding percentages is essential for:
- Calculating discounts and markups in commerce
- Comparing quantities in data analysis
- Solving financial problems (interest, profit, loss)
- Interpreting statistics and survey results

This lesson gives you **five progressive exercises** that build from basic conversion to real-world problem solving.

---

## Exercise 1 — Percentage to Fraction

Convert the following to percentages:

### a) What is 3/4 as a percentage?

**Hint:** Multiply the fraction by 100.

**Solution:**
3/4 × 100 = 75%

### b) Convert 0.65 to percentage

**Hint:** Multiply by 100 and add the % symbol.

**Solution:**
0.65 × 100 = 65%

### Key Concept
To convert a fraction to percentage: (numerator / denominator) × 100

---

## Exercise 2 — Finding a Percentage

What is 20% of 250?

**Approach:**
1. 20% = 20/100 = 0.20
2. Multiply: 250 × 0.20

**Solution:**
250 × 0.20 = 50

### Key Concept
A% of B = (A/100) × B

---

## Exercise 3 — Percentage of a Group

In a class of 50 students, 60% are girls. How many boys are there?

**Approach:**
1. If 60% are girls, then boys = 100% - 60% = 40%
2. Find 40% of 50

**Solution:**
Boys = 40% of 50 = 0.40 × 50 = 20

### Key Concept
If one group is X%, the remainder is (100 - X)%

---

## Exercise 4 — Percentage Change

A shirt costs ₹800 and its price increases to ₹1000. Find the percentage increase.

**Approach:**
1. Calculate the change: New Price - Original Price
2. Divide change by original price
3. Multiply by 100

**Solution:**
Change = 1000 - 800 = 200
% increase = (200 / 800) × 100 = 25%

### Key Concept
% Change = ((Final - Initial) / Initial) × 100

---

## Exercise 5 — Reverse Percentage

A number increases by 25% to become 150. What was the original number?

**Approach:**
1. If the original was 100%, after 25% increase it became 125%
2. So, 150 corresponds to 125%
3. Find 1%: 150 / 125 = 1.2
4. Original (100%) = 1.2 × 100

**Solution:**
Original = 150 × 100/(100 + 25) = 150 × 100/125 = 120

### Key Concept
Original = Final Amount × 100/(100 + % change)

---

## Formulas

- A% of B = (A/100) × B
- % Change = ((Final - Initial) / Initial) × 100
- Original = Final × 100/(100 + % change)
- If X% is one part, remainder = (100 - X)%

## Practice Ideas

- Convert common fractions: 1/4 = 25%, 1/5 = 20%, 3/4 = 75%
- Use 10% as a reference point (easiest percentage to find)
- Practice with real-life examples: sales, discounts, tax
