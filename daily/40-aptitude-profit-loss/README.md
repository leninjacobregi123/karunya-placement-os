# Simple and Compound Interest

**Duration:** 30 minutes  **Difficulty:** Beginner  **Track:** Aptitude + Reasoning

**Learning Goal:** Learn to calculate simple interest, compound interest, and compare them. These are foundational finance concepts frequently tested in placement exams.

## What You'll Learn

Interest is the cost of borrowing money or the return on invested money. Two main types appear in placement tests:

1. **Simple Interest (SI):** Interest calculated only on the original principal amount.
2. **Compound Interest (CI):** Interest calculated on both the principal AND the accumulated interest — it "compounds" over time.

Understanding the difference is critical because compound interest grows faster than simple interest, and the difference widens over time.

---

## Exercise 1 — Simple Interest

Find the simple interest on ₹5000 at 8% per annum for 3 years.

**Approach:**
1. Identify the principal (P), rate (R), and time (T).
2. Use the SI formula.
3. Calculate carefully.

**Solution:**
- Principal (P) = ₹5000
- Rate (R) = 8% per annum
- Time (T) = 3 years

SI = P × R × T / 100
   = 5000 × 8 × 3 / 100
   = ⁠40,000 / 100
   = 1200

Simple interest = ₹1200

### Key Concept
SI = (P × R × T) / 100 — interest is calculated ONLY on the original principal every year.

## Exercise 2 — Compound Interest (Amount)

Find the amount if ₹10000 is compounded annually at 10% for 2 years.

**Approach:**
1. Year 1: Interest = P × R / 100. New amount = P + Interest.
2. Year 2: Use the result from Year 1 as the new principal.
3. Alternatively, use the compound formula directly.

**Solution (Formula method):**
A = P(1 + R/100)^T
  = 10000 × (1 + 10/100)^2
  = 10000 × (1.10)^2
  = 10000 × 1.21
  = 12100

Compound interest earned = A - P = 12100 - 10000 = ₹2100

### Key Concept
Amount = Principal × (1 + Rate/100)^Years

---

## Exercise 3 — Find Compound Interest

Find the compound interest on ₹8000 at 5% for 2 years compounded annually.

**Approach:**
1. Calculate the amount first using the formula A = P(1 + R/100)^T
2. Subtract principal from amount to get CI

**Solution:**
A = 8000 × (1 + 5/100)^2
  = 8000 × (1.05)^2
  = 8000 × 1.1025
  = 8820

CI = 8820 - 8000 = ₹820

### Key Concept
Compound Interest = Amount - Principal. Always calculate the amount first.

---

## Exercise 4 — Reverse Calculation

Find the principal that amounts to ₹13200 in 2 years at 10% simple interest.

**Approach:**
1. Use the SI amount formula: A = P(1 + RT/100)
2. Solve for P.

**Solution:**
A = P(1 + RT/100)
13200 = P(1 + 10×2/100)
13200 = P(1 + 0.2)
13200 = P × 1.2
P = 13200 / 1.2 = 11000

Principal = ₹11000

### Key Concept
For reversed SI: Principal = Amount / (1 + RT/100)

---

## Exercise 5 — CI vs SI Difference

What is the difference between compound interest and simple interest on ₹10000 at 10% for 2 years?

**Approach:**
- Calculate SI: 10000 × 10 × 2 / 100 = 2000
- Calculate CI: 10000 × (1.1)^2 - 10000 = 2100
- Difference = 2100 - 2000 = 100

### Key Concept
For 2 years: Difference = P × (R/100)^2 = 10000 × 0.01 = 100. This shortcut works only for exactly 2 years.

### Key Concept
Compound interest always exceeds simple interest (for R > 0 and T > 1 year) because CI earns interest on interest.

---

## Formulas

- Simple Interest (SI) = P × R × T / 100
- Amount with SI = P + SI = P(1 + RT/100)
- Compound Amount (annual) = P × (1 + R/100)^T
- Compound Interest = Amount - Principal
- Difference (CI - SI) for 2 years = P × (R/100)^2
