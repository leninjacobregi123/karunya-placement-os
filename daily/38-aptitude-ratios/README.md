# Ratios and Proportions

**Duration:** 30 minutes  **Difficulty:** Beginner  **Track:** Aptitude + Reasoning

**Learning Goal:** Learn to compare quantities using ratios, simplify them, and solve problems involving proportions.

## What You'll Learn

Ratios are a way of comparing two quantities. For example, if a class has 15 boys and 10 girls, the ratio of boys to girls is 15:10, which simplifies to 3:2.

Ratios are used everywhere:
- Cooking: recipes use ratios of flour to water
- Maps: map scales are ratios (1 cm on map = 1 km on ground)
- Finance: debt-to-income ratios, interest rate comparisons
- Chemistry: mixing solutions in specific proportions

In this lesson, you'll solve 5 exercises building from basic simplification to real proportion problems.

---

## Exercise 1 — Simplifying Ratios

Simplify the ratio 24:36 to its simplest form.

**Approach:**
1. Find the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) of 24 and 36
2. Divide both terms by the GCD

**Solution:**
GCD(24, 36) = 12
24/12 : 36/12 = 2 : 3

### Key Concept
Divide both terms of a ratio by their GCD to simplify.

---

## Exercise 2 — Solving Proportions

If a:b = 5:3 and a = 25, find b.

**Approach:**
1. Write the proportion: 5/3 = 25/b
2. Cross multiply: 5 × b = 3 × 25
3. Solve for b

**Solution:**
5b = 75
b = 75/5 = 15

### Key Concept
In any proportion a/b = c/d: a × d = b × c (cross product is equal).

---

## Exercise 3 — Ratio Shares

Two numbers are in the ratio 3:4 and their sum is 63. Find the larger number.

**Approach:**
1. If ratio is 3:4, let the numbers be 3x and 4x
2. Add them: 3x + 4x = 7x
3. Set equal to sum: 7x = 63
4. Solve for x, then find the larger (4x)

**Solution:**
3x + 4x = 63
7x = 63
x = 9
Larger = 4 × 9 = 36

### Key Concept
Use a variable (x) to represent the ratio "unit." Total ratio parts × x = actual sum.

---

## Exercise 4 — Continued Proportion

If x:12 = 9:x, find x.

**Approach:**
1. Write: x/12 = 9/x
2. Cross multiply: x × x = 12 × 9
3. Solve: x² = 108

**Solution:**
x² = 108
x = √108 = 6√3 (or approximately 6 × 1.732 = 10.39)

### Key Concept
When the terms form a:b = c:d, use ad = bc to find the unknown.

---

## Exercise 5 — Mixture Problem

A milk mixture has a milk-to-water ratio of 5:3. If the total mixture is 40 liters, find the amount of water.

**Approach:**
1. Milk = 5x, Water = 3x
2. Total = 5x + 3x = 8x
3. 8x = 40 liters
4. Water = 3x

**Solution:**
5x + 3x = 40
8x = 40
x = 5
Water = 3 × 5 = 15 liters

### Key Concept
Total parts = sum of all ratio parts. Each part's share = (ratio share / total parts) × actual total.

---

## Formulas

- Simplify ratio a:b: divide both by GCD(a,b)
- Proportion a:b = c:d means ad = bc
- Share from ratio a:b:c: share = (individual ratio / sum of ratios) × total
