# Peer-Pod Collaboration Guide

## Study Pods in KPOS

Form a pod of 3-4 students who commit to working on KPOS with you. Pods boost accountability and make learning better.

## Daily Pod Routine (2 hours total)

### 1. Morning Check-in (15 min)
Each student shares:
- What they did yesterday
- What their goal is today
- One question or doubt they have

### 2. Individual Work (45 min)
Students work in silence on their assigned KPOS path (30 min) + review yesterday (15 min).

### 3. Peer Review (30 min)
- Each student shows their best answer from today
- Others give feedback: is the code efficient? Is the reasoning correct?
- Discuss mistakes logged from yesterday

### 4. Mock Quiz (30 min)
Rotate who picks the quiz questions. Take turns answering and grading.

## Weekly Pod Activities

- **Monday**: Review last week's mistakes together
- **Wednesday**: One student teaches a concept they just learned
- **Friday**: Full mock test (all do it at the same time, graded peer-to-peer)

## Pod Rules

1. **Show up daily** — if you can't, tell the pod before 8 AM
2. **No excuses** — if the system gives a problem, solve it
3. **Help without giving answers** — the same AI safety policy applies in the pod
4. **Be honest about gaps** — saying "I don't understand" is better than pretending

## Pod Leader (Rotating Weekly)

The pod leader:
- Keeps the morning check-in under 15 min
- Ensures everyone participates
- Reports to faculty (one-line: "all good" or "X is struggling with Y")
